About

My name is Aakriti Shrestha, and I am a proud Culinary Management student at Durham College, Whitby. I am from the breathtakingly beautiful country of Nepal, often called the “Land of the Mountains,” a place where food is not just sustenance but a vibrant celebration of culture, heritage, and togetherness.

My culinary journey began at an early age, inspired by the warmth and creativity of my grandmother and mother. Watching them transform simple ingredients into extraordinary meals ignited my passion for cooking. Singing and playing the guitar while helping in the kitchen became my happy place, instilling in me a deep love for food and the joy it brings to others.

In Nepal, I pursued high school in hotel management, where I immersed myself in the fundamentals of baking, multi-cuisine cooking, guest services, and menu planning. I had the privilege of interning at Hotel Shanker, one of Kathmandu’s prestigious 4-star hotels, where I gained hands-on experience in food production, front office operations, and food & beverage services. This exposure to the hospitality industry fueled my ambition and broadened my culinary perspective.

Moving to Canada has been a life changing chapter. Here, I have access to state of the art tools and modern culinary techniques that were unavailable back home. This has been both a challenge and an opportunity, as I continually push myself to grow and embrace new ways of thinking about food. I am especially passionate about discovering and mastering international cuisines while finding creative ways to celebrate the unique flavours of my Nepali heritage.

Cooking, for me, is more than just a career it is an art form, a way to connect with people and create lasting memories. My dream is to bring Nepali cuisine to the global stage, blending tradition with innovation. I aspire to champion sustainable practices, promote ethical food production, and one day establish my own restaurant or catering service, focusing on both creativity and cultural authenticity. With every dish I create, I aim to honor my roots, inspire others, and make the world just a little more delicious.

Philosophy

Aakriti Shrestha, a 21-year-old culinary student from Nepal, the land of breathtaking mountains and diverse cultural cuisines. Growing up in Nepal, I was surrounded by the rich flavors and traditions of a country where every dish has a story. From the comforting aroma of dal-bhat in family kitchens to the vibrant spices of street-side momos, I discovered early on that food is more than just nourishment, it is a way of expressing culture, bringing people together, and creating memories. In August 2023, I took a bold step and moved to Canada to pursue my passion for cooking and learning. Over the past year and a half, I’ve been exploring a whole new world of culinary techniques, flavors, and traditions. This journey has been an incredible opportunity to grow, not just as a chef but also as a person. Canada’s multicultural food scene has inspired me to think creatively and approach cooking with an open mind. My culinary philosophy is rooted in three principles: culture, creativity, and connection. Culture is where my journey begins. I believe every dish should honor its origins and reflect the heritage it represents. Creativity allows me to reimagine these traditions and bring my unique touch to them, blending flavors and techniques from around the world. Finally, connection is at the heart of everything I do. For me, food is about creating experiences that bring people together, whether it’s a simple meal shared with loved ones or a carefully crafted dish that tells a story. As I continue my studies at Durham College, I am determined to grow as a chef who respects the past, embraces innovation, and prioritizes sustainability in the kitchen. My goal is to blend the rich flavors of my Nepalese heritage with everything I’ve learned abroad, creating dishes that inspire joy, comfort, and curiosity. Cooking, for me, is not just about making food it’s about creating a sense of belonging and building bridges between cultures.

Recipe

Seared Sea Bream with Accompaniments

Ingredients

  • For Sea Bream:
    • 225 g sea bream fillet (1 per portion)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 10 g butter
    • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
    • 2 scallops (seared, for garnish)
    • 10 g crisp capers
    • Basil-Parsley Oil (for drizzling)
  • For Horseradish-Chive Mashed Potatoes:
    • 250 g Yukon Gold potatoes (peeled)
    • 20 g butter
    • 10 ml Dijon mustard
    • 35 g horseradish
    • 10 g chopped fresh chives
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For Vegetables:
    • 150 g carrots (peeled, cut on a bias)
    • 100 g spinach (washed)
    • 15 g butter
    • 5 ml Dijon mustard
    • 5 ml honey
    • 5-10 ml vegetable oil
    • 5 ml salt
    • 2.5 ml pepper
    • 2 shallots (peeled and diced)
  • For Basil-Parsley Oil:
    • 20 ml parsley oil
    • 30 ml basil oil
    • 50 ml vegetable oil

Procedure

  • Prepare Sea Bream:
    • Scale and fillet the sea bream. Score the skin with 3 scores per fillet.
    • Season with salt and pepper.
    • Sear in a pan with vegetable oil over medium-high heat, 2 minutes per side.
    • Keep warm.
  • Prepare Horseradish-Chive Mashed Potatoes:
    • Boil peeled potatoes until tender.
    • Drain, rest for 2-3 minutes, and mash.
    • Mix in butter, Dijon mustard, horseradish, chives, and seasonings.
  • Prepare Vegetables:
    • Blanch carrots for 2-3 minutes.
    • Sauté in butter with mustard, honey, oil, salt, and pepper.
    • Sauté shallots in butter, add spinach, wilt, and season.
  • Prepare Seared Scallops:
    • Dry scallops, coat lightly with mycryo cocoa butter.
    • Sear in a hot cast iron skillet for 2 minutes per side.
  • Prepare Crisp Capers:
    • Toss capers in cornstarch.
    • Fry in hot oil for 1-2 minutes.
    • Drain on paper towels.
  • Prepare Basil-Parsley Oil:
    • Blanch parsley and basil, shock in ice water, dry, and chop.
    • Blend with parsley oil, basil oil, and vegetable oil.
    • Rest for 1 hour and strain.
